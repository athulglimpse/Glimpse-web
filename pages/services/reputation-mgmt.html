Online reputation management (ORM) is the practice of monitoring, influencing, and improving how your brand, business, or personal name appears across search engines, social media platforms, review sites, and news publications. In today's digital landscape, your online reputation is often the first impression a potential customer, investor, or partner will form about you before they ever speak to your team or visit your premises. A single negative news article, a cluster of bad reviews, or damaging social media content can directly impact sales, partnerships, recruitment, and brand trust. ORM ensures your digital presence accurately reflects the quality and credibility of your brand.

Search Engine Reputation Management (SERM) is the practice of actively managing what appears in Google search results when someone searches for your brand, business, or name. When negative content a critical news article, a damaging review, or a competitor's misleading page appears on the first page of Google for your brand name, it directly harms your reputation with every person who searches for you. SERM works by creating, optimising, and promoting high-quality positive content across multiple authoritative platforms including your own website, press releases, social media profiles, guest articles, and business listings so that this content outranks and displaces the negative results over time.

We use a combination of professional monitoring tools and manual review processes to track your brand's online reputation continuously. Crisis reputation management is the rapid response process activated when a significant reputational threat emerges such as a viral negative news story, a social media controversy, a product recall, a data breach, or coordinated negative content campaigns. It requires immediate action: assessing the nature and scale of the threat, defining the response narrative, coordinating communications across owned channels (website, social media, email), engaging media or platforms where necessary, and deploying SERM tactics to protect search result visibility. Glimpse provides crisis reputation management as both a reactive emergency service and a proactive preparedness programme so that when a crisis occurs, your team has a clear playbook rather than improvising under pressure.

Reputation management is a sustained, long-term process not a quick fix. The timeline for visible improvement depends on the severity of the reputational issue and the tactics involved. SERM results displacing negative content from Google's first page generally take 3–6 months for moderate cases and up to 12 months or more for severe or deeply embedded negative content.
