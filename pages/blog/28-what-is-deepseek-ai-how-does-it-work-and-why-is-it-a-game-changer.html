What is DeepSeek AI, How Does It Work, and Why is It a Game Changer?

by Daniel Rafearo | February 1, 2025

The AI landscape is a dynamic terrain, where new models emerge, each a unique point on the cognitive map. This exploration delves into DeepSeek, a Chinese AI presence, charting its course and comparing it to other prominent models like ChatGPT, Gemini, and the vast expanse of open-source alternatives.

Deciphering DeepSeek

DeepSeek, born from a Hangzhou startup, has rapidly ascended in the AI sphere. Its capabilities span text generation, project planning, and even creative composition, mirroring functionalities found in its Western counterparts. Its brief reign as the top free app on the U.S. App Store underscores its disruptive potential.

Navigating the Shadows: Safety Concerns

Safety remains paramount in evaluating any AI. DeepSeek's trajectory has encountered several shadows:

Echoes of Authority: Analyses suggest DeepSeek sometimes amplifies official Chinese narratives without prompting, raising concerns about information control and the blurring of fact and opinion. Tests have indicated a high rate of echoing government positions, even when presented with conflicting information.

Vulnerabilities in the Architecture: Reports of prompt injection vulnerabilities expose potential weaknesses in DeepSeek's defenses, potentially granting malicious actors access and control. This underscores the critical need for robust security in the AI domain.

Data's Horizon: DeepSeek's data residency within China raises questions about user privacy and potential government access, prompting national security reviews in some regions.

A Comparative Cartography: DeepSeek, ChatGPT, Gemini, and the Open-Source Constellation

To gain perspective, let's map DeepSeek against other AI models:

ChatGPT (OpenAI): Developed by OpenAI, ChatGPT is renowned for its conversational prowess and vast dataset. OpenAI employs safety protocols and iterative updates to mitigate risks. As a U.S.-based entity, it operates within a specific regulatory framework, offering a degree of transparency.

Gemini (Google DeepMind): Forged by Google DeepMind, Gemini emphasizes reasoning and problem-solving. Google adheres to ethical AI guidelines, prioritizing safety, fairness, and privacy. Its corporate structure provides a level of oversight.

Open-Source Models: This diverse constellation is shaped by collaborative efforts, with code openly accessible. Safety varies depending on the community's involvement and resources. Transparency is inherent in the open-source ethos, fostering community-driven improvements.

Charting a Course Forward

DeepSeek represents a significant stride in AI, yet its potential must be tempered with awareness of its safety profile. Models like ChatGPT and Gemini offer established safety measures and greater transparency, though they are not without their own challenges. Open-source models provide transparency and communal development, but safety can be inconsistent.

Choosing an AI demands careful consideration of safety, transparency, data privacy, and specific application needs. Continuous learning and rigorous evaluation are essential for responsible and effective AI deployment.
