Twitter advertisers have to pay for verification to launch ads

by Hashim Riyan | February 20, 2023

Twitter, a leading social media platform with over 330 million active users, has been instrumental in helping businesses reach their target audience and promote their products and services. The platform was a crucial advertising tool that allowed brands to create awareness among potential customers and expand their reach. However, Twitter recently announced a significant change that sparked much controversy: Advertisers must now pay for verification before they can start showing ads.

Verification is the process by which Twitter confirms the authenticity of an account owned by a specific person or organization. To do this, Twitter must be provided with proof of identity, e.g. B. an official ID or a business license. Once verified, an account will be marked with a blue badge, indicating it is a legitimate account.

In a sudden and unexpected move, Twitter changed its verification process for advertisers, requiring them to get verified before running ads. Consequently, businesses must go through the verification process and pay a fee to Twitter before they can start advertising on the platform. This action has drawn a backlash from advertisers who feel they are being unfairly coerced into paying for what was once a free service.

The main reason Twitter decided to make this change is to combat the escalating problem of fake accounts and spam on the platform. By requiring advertiser verification, Twitter can ensure that the accounts showing ads are legitimate and are not being used for nefarious purposes. This allows Twitter to improve the overall quality of ads on the platform and establish itself as a more reliable source of information.

Additionally, the policy change aims to increase Twitter's revenue. By charging a fee for verification, Twitter can generate additional revenue from advertisers, a common practice among social media platforms that require businesses to pay for additional features and services.

While the move to require advertisers to pay for verification has sparked controversy, it's important to remember that Twitter is a business and needs to generate revenue to remain profitable. By requiring advertiser verification, Twitter can ensure that the ads on its platform are of high quality and trustworthy. This can benefit both advertisers and users by ensuring that the ads are relevant and useful.

However, it is imperative for Twitter to ensure that the verification process is impartial and transparent. Advertisers should be given clear guidelines on the verification process and associated fees, and the process should be simplified to ensure it is not overly complicated or time-consuming. Twitter should also offer support to advertisers who are new to the platform or unfamiliar with the verification process.

In summary, Twitter's decision to require advertisers to pay for verification before showing ads has caused quite a stir, but it's a necessary step to stop the proliferation of fake accounts and spam on the platform to fight. By ensuring that the accounts running ads are legitimate and trustworthy, Twitter can improve the overall quality of ads on the platform and establish itself as a more reliable source of information. However, Twitter needs to ensure that the review process is transparent and unbiased, and that advertisers are given clear guidelines and support. In this way, Twitter can continue to be an invaluable tool for businesses to reach their audience and promote their brand.
